Learn the 5 stages of Inbound Marketing:

  1. Attract visitors to the website
    • Most users will search for products and services themselves through the following three channels: Google, blogs and social networks. To attract them to your website, you have to rely on many different techniques.
    • Ways to improve website rankings on Google:Choose the perfect keyword, Optimize title tags (title tag), Website content, Blogs, Social networks (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube…)
    • Most users will search for products and services themselves through the following three channels: Google, blogs and social networks. To attract them to your website, you have to rely on many different techniques.
  2. Transformation
    • Motivate people to take action with CTAs (call-to-action) such as: signing up for a newsletter, filling out a registration form or offering to use a trial version... to get them to interact with your business more.
  3. Marketing automation
    • Leadscoring: is the process of assigning value to each potential customer so that the sales team can quickly reach the customers who need to be interacted with first.
    • Leadnurturing: Based on the score achieved and the characteristics of the lead, you will design a series of automated flows to send information relevant to their needs.
  4. Brand loyalty
    • The process isn't over when you convert leads into customers. The next step is to get this customer to stay with you as long as possible through customer loyalty building strategies.
  5. Check progress and measure results
    • For each stage, track your progress. Measure expected customers, potential customers, actual customers on each channel over time, as well as conversion rates...

Strengths of Out-bound?

  • Actively promote your brand's message to your target audience.
  • Outbound Marketing includes different Marketing strategies and techniques that target a wide range of users.
  • Outbound Marketing offers the ability to set targeting limits based on many factors.
  • The budget for this type of campaign is planned from the beginning, which simplifies tracking.

Advantages of using Outbound Marketing:

  1. One of the most valuable aspects of Outbound Marketing is its ability to reach a target.
    • Especially when it comes to online Outbound Marketing.
  2. Outbound Marketing offers the ability to set targeting limits based on many factors.
    • These include demographics, geography, interests and behavior. This allows advertisers to target only the audience of their choice.
  3. Outbound Marketing is also relatively easy to measure effectiveness.
  4. Online outbound marketing is a popular method for generating leads.

Popular types of Outbound Marketing:

  1. Traditional advertising
    • Traditional advertising includes media such as television, radio, print (newspapers, magazines, etc.) and billboards.
    • This method helps you convey your message to the masses.
  2. Digital Ads
    • This is considered a modernized version of media advertising.
    • Digital advertising allows you to reach your target audience on the Internet front.
  3. Cold Calling
    • Staff will make calls based on a database of people who could be potential customers.
    • Salespeople often do not have prior communication with customers, hence the term Cold Calling.

Outbound Marketing may have played a very important role in the past. However, along with the development of the Internet, the shift from traditional forms of Marketing to Inbound Marketing is considered inevitable.

Types of Telemarketing:

  1. Inbound Telemarketing
    • This is a form for actual consumer products, used to record feedback from customers who use the product.

    • Telesales: Call target customers and sell products.
    • Telephone Survey: Investigation and research activities on the consumer market or target market via telephone.
    • Event Broadcasting: Activities carried out for the purpose of communicating upcoming events and campaigns.
